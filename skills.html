I also practiced this skill throughout my education, between projects, deadlines, and scheduling, I had to find the best ways to manage any time I had available to work on assignments and/or projects.

  • Customer Service

    • Throughout both of my jobs, I have had extensive experience with helping customers, and making their experience as pleasant as possible.   • Teamwork

    • I have had extensive experience throughout my life working with teams, as I thrive in a team environment. While working at Walmart, there was never a time when we didn't need to coordinate with our team to make sure we were stocking the sales floor efficiently and properly. We spent time in daily huddles, going over what we were accomplishing, and what may have needed work, in order to improve our productivity as much as possible every day. At Titus-Will Toyota, we work as a team to make sure all the tasks are completed, without making customers wait any longer than necessary. This meant coordinating on who was working on what task, to ensure the most efficient use of our time, and the shortest wait time for customers possible. I have also had plenty of experience with teamwork throughout my education, with some of the most recent examples being, working on team projects in my software development courses, where it is absolutely necessary to coordinate with a team to make sure that tasks and goals are distributed fairly, and frequent meetings to ensure that everyone is on-track. This is extremely relevant to my future career, as it gives very similar experiences to working with a team in the professional software development field.

  • Multitasking

    • Working as a lot porter at Titus-Will Toyota has given me a great deal of experience with multitasking, as it is a necessary trait within the job, balancing between checking customers in, bringing cars to the mechanics, and washing/vacuuming cars before returning them to the customers. I have also had experience multitasking in my education. Some more recent examples of this would be listening to a lecture in class, while simultaneously working on applying the same concepts hands-on in a program, to gain a better understanding of how the concepts work, and to ensure I fully understand how to implement these concepts in a real environment.
